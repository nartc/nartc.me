# Repository guidance

## Blog writing voice

Write blog posts like an experienced engineer explaining something they have actually run into. The voice is practical, conversational, opinionated, and code-driven. Prefer "here is the thing, here is why it bothers me, here is what I would do" over polished devrel prose.

### Do

- Start from a concrete Angular/API/code problem, not a broad motivational intro.
- Use first person when the opinion is subjective: "I think", "Personally", "for me", "I tend to".
- Show the normal/obvious approach before criticizing it.
- Be fair to existing APIs: "This is completely fine" before explaining why it is not the preferred default.
- Use short punchy lines for emphasis.
- Use rhetorical questions to expose design ambiguity: "What does this thing actually do?"
- Keep examples small and focused. Use code to anchor the argument.
- Prefer plain headings that reflect the thought process: `## The problem`, `## Root first`, `## So what would I do?`.
- End with a practical rule of thumb, not a grand thesis.

### Don't

- Do not sound like marketing copy.
- Do not over-polish the author's perspective away.
- Do not use generic phrases like "unlock", "seamless", "powerful", "revolutionary".
- Do not overstate subjective preferences as universal truth.
- Do not skip the awkward/failed/common approach; it is usually the reason the post exists.
- Do not introduce abstractions without showing the pain first.

### Preferred phrasing

- "This is fine."
- "This works, but..."
- "The problem is..."
- "At first glance..."
- "With that in mind..."
- "We're close."
- "This is where it gets awkward."
- "It is not bad, but..."
- "Personally, I prefer..."
- "That is probably the part that bothers me the most."

### Common structure

1. Short context.
2. Code example.
3. Why the common approach is lacking.
4. Refined example or alternative.
5. Caveats/tradeoffs.
6. Practical conclusion.
